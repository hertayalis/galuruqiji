The Meaning Behind The Song: What Goes Up Must Come Down by Ethel Waters

Posted by Martina Birk on Sunday, June 23, 2024

The song “What Goes Up Must Come Down” is a timeless classic that was originally performed by the incomparable Ethel Waters. Written by Harold Arlen and Ted Koehler, this song first graced the airwaves in 1931. It has since become a beloved jazz standard, beloved for its poignant lyrics and beautiful melody.

The meaning behind this song is both simple and profound. At its core, it speaks to the universal truth that everything in life is cyclical. Just as the sun rises and sets, and the tides ebb and flow, our lives are filled with highs and lows. This song serves as a reminder that no matter how high we may soar, we must inevitably come back down to earth.

Frequently Asked Questions about “What Goes Up Must Come Down” by Ethel Waters

1. Who wrote the song “What Goes Up Must Come Down”?

The song was written by Harold Arlen and Ted Koehler. Arlen was a prolific composer and is best known for his work on the Wizard of Oz soundtrack.

2. When was the song released?

“What Goes Up Must Come Down” was originally released in 1931. Ethel Waters recorded the song, and her rendition remains one of the most popular versions to this day.

3. What genre does this song belong to?

This song falls under the jazz genre. It showcases the unique style and vocal prowess of Ethel Waters, who was known for her incredible contributions to jazz and blues music.

4. Are there any notable covers of this song?

Yes, over the years, many artists have covered “What Goes Up Must Come Down.” Some notable versions include performances by Ella Fitzgerald, Billie Holiday, and Frank Sinatra.

5. What is the significance of the song’s lyrics?

The lyrics of “What Goes Up Must Come Down” highlight the transient nature of life and the inevitability of ups and downs. They serve as a reminder to remain grounded and humble, even during the best of times.

6. What makes Ethel Waters’ rendition of the song special?

Ethel Waters’ rendition of “What Goes Up Must Come Down” is widely regarded as the definitive version. Her soulful and emotive delivery brings out the depth and meaning of the lyrics, leaving a lasting impact on listeners.

7. Is there a deeper message in the song?

While the song’s meaning may seem straightforward, there is a deeper message about resilience and embracing the vicissitudes of life. It encourages listeners to find strength in both the highs and lows and to keep moving forward.

8. What instruments are typically featured in this song?

The song is often accompanied by a jazz ensemble, including instruments such as piano, saxophone, trumpet, double bass, and drums. These instruments contribute to the energetic and uplifting nature of the music.

9. How has the song stood the test of time?

“What Goes Up Must Come Down” remains a beloved jazz standard due to its timeless lyrics and memorable melody. It continues to resonate with audiences of all ages, serving as a testament to its enduring appeal.

10. What impact did Ethel Waters have on the music industry?

Ethel Waters was a trailblazing African-American singer and actress who broke barriers with her talent, paving the way for future generations of artists. Her contributions to jazz and blues have left an indelible mark on the music industry.

11. Can this song be interpreted in different ways?

Yes, like any great piece of art, “What Goes Up Must Come Down” can be interpreted in various ways. While the song’s primary message revolves around the cyclical nature of life, individuals may find their own personal meanings within its lyrics.

12. Is there a personal connection between Ethel Waters and the song’s message?

While it is unclear whether Ethel Waters had a personal connection to the song’s message, her masterful delivery suggests that she truly understood and embodied the emotions conveyed in the lyrics.

In conclusion, “What Goes Up Must Come Down” by Ethel Waters is a song that serves as a poignant reminder of life’s cyclical nature. Through its heartfelt lyrics and beautiful melody, it has captivated audiences for decades and continues to be a cherished piece of music history.
